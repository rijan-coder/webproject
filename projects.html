Major Hydropower Initiatives

The development of indigenous hydropower resources is central to Nepal's energy strategy. NEA is currently overseeing several mega-projects aimed at significantly increasing the nation's generating capacity. These projects not only address domestic demand but also position Nepal as a regional energy exporter. Emphasis is placed on run-of-river schemes and reservoir projects to balance seasonal variation in water flow. Environmental and social impact assessments are integral to every phase of planning and construction to ensure sustainable development. We are committed to transparency in project execution and timely completion of all scheduled works.

Key National Priority Projects

  1. Upper Tamakoshi Hydropower Project (456 MW):Recently completed and contributing significantly to the national grid.
  2. *Budhi Gandaki Hydroelectric Project (1200 MW):A multi-purpose reservoir project currently in the land acquisition phase.
  3. Chilime Sanjen Project (42 MW):A vital project for regional power supply and distribution stability.

Transmission Line Expansion
